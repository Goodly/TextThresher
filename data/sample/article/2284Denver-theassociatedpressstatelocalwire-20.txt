Denver police seek $200K for Occupy overtime
SECTION: STATE AND REGIONAL
LENGTH: 130 words
DATELINE: DENVER


<Government>Denver officials are asking the City Council for an extra $200,000 for police overtime through the end of the year because of the Occupy Denver protest. />
<Government>Department of Safety spokeswoman Daelene Mix said Wednesday the request might not cover all the overtime from the protests, but only the amount that isn't in the budget./>
<Government>She says officials are still calculating the amount of overtime police have incurred so far related to the protest./>
<Government>Denver officials said last month that five city departments accumulated $365,000 in overtime over five days of the protest. Mix now says that total included both overtime and regular time./>
<Government>The $200,000 request is part of $7 million that safety officials want for a variety of expenses. The Denver Post reported the total on its website Wednesday./>
