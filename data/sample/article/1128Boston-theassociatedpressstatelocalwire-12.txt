Police investigate graffiti near Occupy Boston
SECTION: STATE AND REGIONAL
LENGTH: 114 words
DATELINE: BOSTON
 
<Camp>Boston police are investigating a rash of graffiti in the city's financial district that appears to be in support of the nearby Occupy Boston movement./>
<Camp>Police say nearly two dozen locations, including banks, were hit over the weekend by spray-painted messages that say things such as "End the Fed" and "Tax the Rich." Some of the graffiti includes a capital A in a circle, a symbol used by anarchists./>
<Camp>Police say they have made no arrests but are looking at surveillance video from the area./>
<Camp>Protesters from Occupy Boston are living in a tent city on the edge of the financial district./>
<Strategy>Some protesters say vandalism is counterproductive to their goals and they don't condone the graffiti./>
